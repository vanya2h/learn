## Cover Letter Draft

---

Building the frontend layer of a DeFi protocol from first principles — owning the architecture, integrating directly with smart contracts, and shipping a product that traders actually trust — is precisely the kind of role I've spent the last seven years preparing for. Ambient Finance caught my attention for that reason.

At Rarible, I joined as a founding engineer and grew with the team from zero to 100K daily active users across five blockchain ecosystems. I built the core marketplace frontend architecture, co-authored the Rarible SDK (adopted by 60+ third-party integrators), and led the frontend org through rapid scaling. The work was TypeScript throughout — wagmi, viem, ethers.js for contract interactions, a BFF layer in Express, and full E2E test coverage in Playwright. More recently at Evergon, I architected a full-stack RWA tokenization platform from scratch — React + Vite + Tailwind on the frontend, Hono.js + PostgreSQL on the backend, ponder.sh for blockchain indexing, and a public SDK for third-party integrators. The protocol has grown to $200M TVL.

Smart contract integrations aren't something I layer on top — they're how I think about DeFi product architecture. I'm fluent in reading protocol state, building reactive UIs around on-chain data, handling async wallet interactions, and designing the abstractions that keep complex DeFi logic approachable for users. TypeScript-first, SOLID-principled, and deeply familiar with the EVM stack that Ambient is built on.

I'm looking for exactly this kind of role: early team, real technical ownership, and a protocol that matters in DeFi. Happy to talk through how I'd approach the engineering challenges at Ambient.

Ivan K.
