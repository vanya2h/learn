## Job Description

> Source: https://jobstash.xyz/senior-software-engineer-tech-lead-web3-initiatives-reap/ghHgLz

Reap is building card-linked stablecoin and crypto payment infrastructure — you own the entire web3 layer. The role covers wallet infrastructure discovery and development for the Card product, production-grade smart contract deployment across Solana and Ethereum, and backend services in Node.js/TypeScript supporting on-chain functionality.

**Responsibilities:**
- Own Reap's web3 capabilities end-to-end — from architecture to deployment
- Design and deploy production-grade smart contracts on Solana (Rust) and Ethereum (Solidity)
- Build backend services in Node.js and TypeScript to support on-chain functionality
- Design and ship wallet infrastructure — hot wallets, cold wallets, stablecoin rails
- Collaborate with product and business teams to translate ideas into secure technical execution
- Contribute to architecture decisions, code reviews, and security audits
- Leverage AI tools and modern practices to increase engineering efficiency

**Requirements:**
- 5–8+ years in software development with backend engineering in Node.js/TypeScript and AWS (or similar cloud)
- 3+ years building smart contracts on Solana (Rust) or Ethereum (Solidity)
- Experience building wallet infrastructure (hot and cold wallets)
- Strong product sense and ability to take end-to-end ownership

**Skills listed:** Multi-Chain, Hot Wallet, Solana, Cross-Chain, Ethereum, Node.js, TypeScript, Architecture, DeFi, AWS, Backend, Cold Storage, Rust, Solidity, Payments, Smart Contract, Security Audit, Card Issuance, Wallet Infrastructure, Cold Wallet, Stablecoin, AI

Two years ago I joined Evergon as the lead engineer to build RWA tokenization infrastructure from zero. I architected the client application, REST APIs, blockchain indexing layer, and a public SDK for third-party integrators — all on top of Ethereum/EVM using TypeScript, Node.js, wagmi, and ponder.sh. The protocol grew to $200M TVL. The Reap Web3 Tech Lead role reads like the same mandate in a different vertical: own the on-chain layer, ship the wallet infrastructure, and make blockchain capabilities real for a product that has significant non-crypto distribution.

My background maps closely to what you need. At Rarible I integrated five blockchain ecosystems — Ethereum, Solana, Tezos, Aptos, Flow — into a unified frontend and co-authored the Rarible SDK adopted by 60+ third-party integrators, while the platform scaled to 100K daily active users. At Evergon I went deeper into the backend and protocol layers: ERC-7208 data standards, smart contract integrations, on-chain indexing with ponder.sh, and a whitelabel SDK for RWA issuers. The hot/cold wallet infrastructure and cross-chain requirements in this role are exactly the surface area I have been working on.

On the TypeScript/Node side: it's been my primary production stack for the past decade. I build backends with Hono.js and Node, ship REST APIs and BFF layers, and maintain full E2E test coverage with Playwright. I'm also deeply integrated with AI tooling as a core part of how I design and ship — it's not a workflow add-on but the default way I operate.

Reap sits at the intersection of crypto infrastructure and real-world financial products — the combination I find most interesting right now. I'd welcome the chance to talk about how I could own and grow the Web3 layer for your Card product.

Ivan K.
