## Cover Letter Draft

---

Ten years building in Web3 — from early Ethereum ICO infrastructure at Daonomic in 2017, through scaling Rarible to 100K daily active users across five blockchains, to leading the build of RWA tokenization infrastructure at Evergon that reached $200M TVL — has given me a consistent point of view: the best crypto products are the ones that make complex on-chain mechanics invisible to the people using them. That's the engineering challenge I want to be working on, and Tether's platform sits at the center of it.

On the fullstack side, TypeScript has been my primary language for close to a decade, across React frontends, Node.js backends, BFF layers, and SDKs. At Rarible I owned the frontend architecture for a marketplace with 60+ third-party integrators and co-authored the Rarible SDK used across five blockchain ecosystems. At Evergon I took the full stack further — building investor panels, issuer APIs, a public SDK, and blockchain indexing infrastructure using wagmi, viem, ponder.sh, and Hono.js. I've also shipped a Top 10 App Store React Native app (Glowbe, Health & Fitness) and built WebRTC/WebSockets live streaming systems — the real-time and P2P surface area your infrastructure touches.

What draws me specifically to Tether is the scale and the infrastructure-level nature of the work. USDT underpins a huge portion of global crypto activity — the products built on top of it need to be reliable, performant, and maintainable across a very broad user base. That's the type of engineering environment where I do my best work.

I'd be glad to talk through how my background maps to where your team is building right now.

Ivan K.
